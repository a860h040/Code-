TITLE: Contract Price Integrity: Using Vizient Catalog & Price Verification to Prevent Overpayment INTRO: Hospitals can lose money when the price on an invoice is different from the contracted price. This topic explains how teams can use contract and pricing tools to find “pricing leakage” early and fix it with less manual work. BODY Common reasons pricing is incorrect The wrong contract tier is applied. The unit of measure (UOM) is wrong (for example, “each” vs “box”). The wholesaler’s pricing file is not updated. A product is substituted or packaged differently than expected. A simple, clean workflow 1. Find the mismatch (invoice price does not match contract price). 2. Confirm the correct contract terms (tier, UOM, contract coverage). 3. Escalate to the right contact (wholesaler, vendor, internal buyer). 4. Request and track credits (recover the overpaid amount). 5. Fix the root cause so it does not happen again. What to measure Mismatch rate (how often prices do not match) Dollars recovered ($ recovered) Time to fix each issue (time-to-resolution) Top causes (UOM errors, tier errors, missing contract coverage) VIZIENT IMPACT Vizient Catalog Vizient Catalog helps as a “source of truth” for product details, pricing, and contract information. This helps members know what they should be paying. Pharmacy Price Verification Pharmacy Price Verification supports centralized monitoring. It helps members find incorrect charges for Vizient-contracted products and recover credits. New drug launches, inflation, shortages, and changes in how drugs are used can all impact spending. This topic explains a clear forecasting method that pharmacy leaders can trust. BODY Main reasons budgets miss the target New product launches (new drugs added to the market) Biosimilars (switching to lower-cost alternatives) Shortage substitutions (using different drugs because the usual drug is unavailable) Contract changes (new pricing or contract terms) Mix shift (using more of certain drugs than expected) Site-of-care shifts (moving treatment to a different setting, like inpatient vs outpatient) A simple forecasting workflow 1. Start with baseline spend (what you usually spend). 2. Adjust using market signals (expected changes from the market). 3. Build scenarios: best case / base case / worst case. 4. Review monthly results and explain the variance (difference between forecast and actual spend). KPIs to track Forecast accuracy (how close the forecast is to real spend) Top variance drivers (the main reasons the forecast was off) “Early warning” thresholds (signals that spending may change soon) VIZIENT IMPACT Pharmacy Market Outlook Pharmacy Market Outlook shares market factors that may affect purchasing patterns over the next 6 to 18 months. Drug Budget Forecast (DBF) Drug Budget Forecast helps create line-item spend projections based on a member’s spending mix. It supports budgeting and longer-term planning. Benchmarking idea (compare similar members) Compare forecast accuracy and top variance drivers across similar groups, such as members with the same size or similar service lines.
