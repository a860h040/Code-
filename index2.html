**TITLE:** 340B Strategy Under Pressure — Staying Compliant, Protecting Savings, and Using Analytics to Show Value **INTRO:** The 340B Program can save hospitals a lot of money. But audits and scrutiny are increasing. Many health systems struggle to balance compliance, contracts, and daily workflow. **BODY:** **Where savings can get lost:** * Duplicate discounts (getting the same discount twice by mistake) * Contract pharmacy complexity (many moving parts and rules) * Mixed-use inventory (340B and non-340B drugs getting mixed together) * Documentation gaps (missing or unclear records) **What to track and benchmark:** * Eligible claims capture rate (how many eligible claims you actually capture) * Audit findings (problems found during audits) * Split-billing exception rate (how often the billing system has errors/exceptions) * Savings vs leakage (how much you save vs how much you lose from mistakes) **Governance model (how to manage it well):** * Regular compliance checks * Standard reports on a set schedule (weekly/monthly) **VIZIENT IMPACT:** Vizient advisory and analytics can help members: * Standardize 340B reporting across sites * Find “leakage” (where savings are being lost) * Compare performance to similar organizations (peer benchmarking) **Suggested deliverables:** * A member-ready 340B KPI report (easy to share with leadership) * An “audit readiness” checklist (what to have prepared before an audit) **CONCLUSION:** **Deliverable:** 340B dashboard template + audit readiness checklist. **Engagement idea:** a short “spot the leakage” mini-case activity. **REFERENCE LINKS:** * HRSA 340B program overview: [https://www.hrsa.gov/opa](https://www.hrsa.gov/opa) * Apexus 340B University (tools/guidance): [https://www.340bpvp.com/340b-university/](https://www.340bpvp.com/340b-university/) **TITLE:** Pharmacy Contract Compliance & Standard Use — How Networks Improve Market Share and Save Money **INTRO:** A good pharmacy contract does not help if people do not use it correctly. This topic focuses on a repeatable process to improve contract compliance and reduce variation across sites. **BODY:** **Why compliance drops:** * NDC variation (same drug, different NDCs that are not on contract) * Substitutions (switching products that are not contracted) * Prescriber preference (providers choose different brands/products) * Formulary drift (the formulary changes over time without control) * Contract gaps (needed items are not covered by the GPO contract) **What to measure:** * Contract market share (how much you buy on contract vs total) * Off-contract spend (money spent outside the contract) * Top “leakage” items (items causing the most off-contract spend) * Conversion rate after interventions (how many switch back after changes) **How to improve compliance (change tactics):** * Align order sets and formulary (make the right product the default) * Add purchasing controls (limit non-contract options when appropriate) * Build exception workflows (clear rules for when off-contract is allowed) * Use feedback loops (share results with sites and prescribers regularly) **VIZIENT IMPACT:** Vizient Pharmacy Networks and analytics can help members: * Benchmark contract market share and compliance across peers * Support standardization across multiple hospitals or sites **Suggested deliverables:** * Contract compliance playbook (step-by-step process) * Starter KPI definitions for cross-member comparison (same metrics, same rules) **CONCLUSION:** **Deliverable:** Compliance scorecard + top 10 leakage action plan. **Engagement idea:** Quick poll: “What is the biggest barrier to compliance?” **REFERENCE LINKS:** * Vizient Pharmacy (overview): Growth & Financial Impact — Using Data to Improve Access, Adherence, and Total Cost of Care **INTRO:** Specialty pharmacy is growing quickly and drives a large part of drug spending. The challenge is how to grow programs while proving outcomes and keeping patient access realistic. **BODY:** **Key decisions and challenges:** * Build vs partner (run your own specialty pharmacy or work with an outside partner) * Site-of-care shifts (moving therapy from hospital/infusion center to home or outpatient settings) * Payer requirements and prior authorization workload (more rules, more paperwork, more delays) **What to measure (core KPIs):** * Referral capture rate (how many referrals you keep in your program) * Time-to-therapy (how long it takes to start treatment) * Abandonment rate (how often patients do not start after approval/prescription) * Adherence (PDC = proportion of days covered) * Total cost signals (changes in hospitalizations, ED visits, overall spend trends) **Operational analytics (how to find problems):** * Where the bottlenecks are in the process * Staffing productivity (work volume per staff time) * Turnaround time for key steps (intake → benefits → PA → fill → delivery) * Top denial reasons (why payers say “no”) **VIZIENT IMPACT:** Vizient pharmacy advisory and analytics can help members: * Build standardized specialty pharmacy metrics * Compare performance across similar organizations (peer benchmarking) **Suggested deliverables:** * Specialty pharmacy KPI dashboard template * “Bottleneck” workflow map (shows where referrals get delayed) **CONCLUSION:** **Deliverable:** Specialty KPI pack + workflow improvement checklist. **Engagement idea:** Short scenario: “Where does the referral get stuck?” **REFERENCE LINKS:** * AMCP specialty medication resources: [https://www.amcp.org/](https://www.amcp.org/) * Pharmacy Times (specialty pharmacy trends hub): [https://www.pharmacytimes.com/](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/)
